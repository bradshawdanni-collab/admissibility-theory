# Divergence Register

This register is a derived artifact built from accepted reviews.

It is not a primary source.
It is not doctrine.
It is a structured aggregation of review outcomes.

The register may be recomputed if the aggregation method changes.
The underlying reviews remain the primary evidence.

## Definitions

- **Interpretive divergence**: reviewers differ in how a term, question, or boundary condition is interpreted.
- **Classification divergence**: reviewers share the same interpretation but reach different classifications.
- **Convergence**: reviewers share the same interpretation and the same classification.
- **Coverage**: how often a term appears in submitted reviews. Low divergence with low coverage is not evidence of stability.
- **Stability ratio**: a summary indicator derived from convergences relative to total review appearances for a term. It should not be interpreted without coverage.

## Governance Boundary

The divergence register records patterns across observations.

It does not itself revise doctrine.

Any doctrine change requires a separate governance process supported by admissible evidence.
