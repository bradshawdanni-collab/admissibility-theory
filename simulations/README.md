# Simulations

The simulations layer provides computational demonstrations of the doctrine.

Simulations exist to demonstrate the doctrine, not to replace it. The constitutional layer is the primary contribution. Simulations are populated last, after the constitutional and doctrine layers are stable and the formalism is sufficiently developed to specify what the simulations should demonstrate.
