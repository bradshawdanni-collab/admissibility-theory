# The One-Page Doctrine

> **A claim earns the right to compete by specifying what would defeat it.**

The One-Page Doctrine is the operational form of the Four Axioms. It translates the constitutional conditions into six questions that can be applied to any claim, in any domain, in a single sitting.

---

## The Six Questions

**1. What is the state space?**
What objects does this framework describe? What is the domain of the claim — the set of things it is making assertions about?

*A claim without a specified state space has not yet determined what it is a claim about.*

---

**2. How are differences distinguished?**
How are differences between states measured or identified? What makes two states of the described system distinguishable from each other?

*This generalizes across domains: in physics, a metric; in law, a standard of evidence; in governance, a decision procedure. The question is the same — what counts as different?*

---

**3. What is conserved?**
What remains invariant under the operations the framework permits? What is the framework committed to preserving across transformations?

*Identifying what is conserved often reveals the framework's actual claims more clearly than its stated conclusions.*

---

**4. What converges?**
Under what conditions does the framework predict stability or a fixed point? If the framework describes a process, where does that process terminate, and under what conditions?

*A framework that predicts convergence without specifying conditions has not made a prediction. It has expressed a hope.*

---

**5. What is ruled out?**
What observation is inconsistent with this framework but consistent with a competitor? This is Axiom 1 — Discriminability — in operational form.

*This is the most important question. If it cannot be answered, the framework is not yet in the domain of science. It is in the domain of motivated architecture.*

---

**6. What breaks the chain?**
At what point in the inference from evidence to conclusion could a third party diverge, and why? Where is the chain of custody most vulnerable?

*Identifying the weakest link is not an admission of failure. It is the most honest signal of where the next work should go.*

---

## Admission Outcomes

| Result | Meaning |
|---|---|
| All six answered | Admitted |
| Five of six answered | Conditionally admitted — specific deficiency identified |
| Three to four answered | Formal hypothesis stage — work in progress |
| One to two answered | Motivated analogy or informal hypothesis |
| None answered | Conceptual sketch |

The output of applying these six questions is always a *location on the maturity spectrum* (see `maturity_spectrum.md`), not a verdict on correctness.

---

## Why Six Questions, Not More

The six questions are minimal. They are the minimum set required to determine whether a claim is in the domain of science rather than the domain of assertion.

Additional evaluation criteria — elegance, parsimony, predictive power, empirical accuracy — are legitimate and important. They are not admissibility criteria. They operate *after* admission, not before it.

The doctrine is a gate, not a ranking. Keep it minimal or it stops being a gate and becomes a preference.
