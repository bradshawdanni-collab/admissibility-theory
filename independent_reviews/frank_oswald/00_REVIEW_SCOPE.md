# Review Scope

## Objective

Evaluate whether the submitted research framework is:

- internally coherent;
- explicitly bounded;
- operationally testable;
- separable from unsupported claims;
- auditable by an independent reader; and
- capable of producing determinate acceptance, rejection, silence, or recovery outcomes.

## Reviewer authority

The reviewer may:

- identify contradictions, ambiguities, missing definitions, circular reasoning, unsupported inferences, and implementation risks;
- disagree materially with the framework;
- recommend rejection, revision, validation work, or narrower claims;
- distinguish conceptual merit from demonstrated evidence.

The reviewer is not being asked to endorse the author, promote the project, or resolve disputed matters in the author's favour.

## Exclusions

The review does not establish:

- empirical efficacy where no experiment has been completed;
- legal, medical, engineering, or regulatory certification;
- ownership or priority beyond the supplied provenance record;
- correctness merely because the material is internally consistent.

## Evidence standard

A finding should cite the relevant file and section. Where possible, use:

`filename — heading, page, paragraph, equation, or line`

Unsupported impressions should be marked as impressions rather than findings.

## Independence condition

The reviewer should complete the declaration before submitting the evaluation. Any prior involvement discovered during review must be disclosed.
