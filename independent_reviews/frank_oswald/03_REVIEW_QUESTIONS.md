# Independent Review Questions

## A. Scope and claims

1. Is the central problem stated clearly?
2. Are the project's claims and non-claims distinguishable?
3. Are any statements framed more strongly than the evidence supports?

## B. Definitions and internal coherence

4. Are the principal terms operationally defined?
5. Do the equations, rules, diagrams, and prose describe the same system?
6. Are there contradictions between constitutional rules and operational procedures?

## C. Identifiability and circularity

7. Can the system determine whether the relevant state is identifiable without using the policy being evaluated?
8. Are thresholds and failure criteria defined before outcomes are observed?
9. Could the framework reject valid reality because of an incomplete or incorrect invariant?

## D. Evidence and falsifiability

10. What observation would count against the framework?
11. Are proposed tests capable of producing failure, not merely confirmation?
12. Are conceptual examples clearly separated from empirical validation?

## E. Governance and authority

13. Are inference, permission, execution, audit, and recovery assigned to distinct roles?
14. Does missing or degraded evidence contract authority deterministically?
15. Are refusal and safe-state transitions operational rather than rhetorical?

## F. Recovery and change control

16. Are recovery conditions measurable and independently verifiable?
17. Can a corrupted reference state be prevented from certifying its own recovery?
18. Are revisions separated from the frozen review object?

## G. Overall determination

19. What is the strongest defensible contribution?
20. What is the most serious unresolved weakness?
21. Select one determination:

- [ ] Accept as internally coherent conceptual architecture
- [ ] Accept with minor corrections
- [ ] Major revision required
- [ ] Insufficient evidence for evaluation
- [ ] Reject on structural grounds

22. State the reason for the determination in no more than 300 words.
