# Independent Review — Start Here

This page is the canonical entry point for an independent review of **Admissibility Theory**.

## Review object

Review the doctrine and supporting material as they existed at the following frozen snapshot:

- **Repository:** `bradshawdanni-collab/admissibility-theory`
- **Frozen doctrine commit:** [`cb98246aff8c8a22549cd442a9e9af907f64b04a`](https://github.com/bradshawdanni-collab/admissibility-theory/tree/cb98246aff8c8a22549cd442a9e9af907f64b04a)
- **Version stated in the doctrine:** `CIL-000.2`

The reviewer-orientation documents added after that snapshot are procedural only. They do not modify the doctrine being evaluated.

## Independence rule

Before submitting the review:

- do not seek explanations, defenses, or reinterpretations of the framework from the author;
- limit questions to file access, navigation, or template mechanics;
- record ambiguity as a finding rather than asking the author to resolve it;
- use your own reasoning and wording;
- disclose any prior familiarity, collaboration, conflict, or relevant limitation.

Disagreement is not a review failure. A clear disagreement is useful evidence.

## Review task

Evaluate the project at two levels:

1. **Framework level:** Is Admissibility Theory stated clearly enough to function as an entry-condition framework rather than a truth-ranking procedure?
2. **Operational level:** Can an independent reader apply the four axioms and six questions consistently without author intervention?

The review should examine:

- clarity and necessity of each axiom;
- whether the axioms are jointly sufficient for the claimed purpose;
- whether discriminability is meaningfully distinct from ordinary falsifiability;
- whether key terms are operationally specified;
- whether the scope boundary is stable and consistently respected;
- whether the six-question procedure yields reproducible classifications;
- whether the worked examples fairly apply the framework;
- whether the maturity claims in `STATUS.md` are warranted;
- the most important ambiguity, counterexample, or disagreement you identify.

## What is outside the required scope

The review does not require:

- accepting the framework's conclusions;
- validating every possible application domain;
- reviewing unrelated AURUM-V or other repository material;
- treating conceptual completeness as empirical validation;
- evaluating implementation progress as proof of theoretical validity.
